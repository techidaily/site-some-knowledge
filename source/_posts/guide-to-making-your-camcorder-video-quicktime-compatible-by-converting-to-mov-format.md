### Video Formats Supported by Camcorder

Different from digital camera that records images in a single file format JPEG, digital camcorder records video in a number of different file formats. So it is important to be aware of these formats, two of which that are commonly supported by camcorder are AVCHD and MOD videos, since they are closed bound up with the problem mentioned above. 

**AVCHD:** a format for recording and playback of high definition video found on most camcorders like  
**Canon** (HF10/HF11/HF200),  
 **JVC** (GZ-HD10/HD30/HD300/HM200/X900),   
 **Sony** (HDR-SR1/SR5C/SR11/TG7/CX12/XR500V),   
 **Panasonic** (HDC-HS200/DMC-GH1/HDC-TM30/HDC-TM350), etc.   
 AVCHD camcorders can capture very high quality video. Its video often compressed in MPEG-4 AVC/H.264 format, and audios are recorded in Dolby Digital. So it is wise to convert AVCHD video to MOV first in case you want to transcode camcorder videos to MOV format.

**MOD:** an informal name of tapeless video formats used by JVC, Panasonic and Canon in some models of digital camcorders. Its format name corresponds to extensions of video files. MOD is used exclusively for standard definition video files. In the similar way, you can first [convert MOD video to MP4](https://tools.techidaily.com/macxdvd/products/) MOV if you camcorder videos come with .mod extension.
